Create the listing for your opportunity

Tell us about who you are looking for

This will show on the opportunity listing under am I the right person for this?

Guidance

You need to include:

  • any required experience, qualification or training
  • the type of person you are looking for
  • any age limitations
  • anything relating to the opportunity that might impact people with access needs
  • if the opportunity requires access to an electronic device and the level of skills needed
Example

This opportunity will suit someone friendly, approachable and enthusiastic.

You don't need any previous experience, qualifications or training. All you need is to:

  • have good people skills and want to chat with people
  • be reliable and be good at time keeping
  • enjoy the outdoors
  • be 16 or over
  • you need access to a telephone (for remote opportunities)

This opportunity requires some walking. If this doesn't suit you, we can discuss other ways you can volunteer with us.

Add an overview of the application process

This will show on the opportunity listing under what happens after you register with us.

Guidance

You need to include:

  • how and when you'll get in touch with the applicant
  • what happens after
  • a list of the checks they will have to go through
Example for a specific opportunity

We'll get in touch with you when we review your request. This might take a few weeks.

After that, we may book in a chat to get to know you a bit more and walk you through the process.

Example for a generic opportunity

We'll get in touch with you when we review your request. This might take a few weeks.

After that, we will get in touch with you to discuss which roles are available and how you may support us.

We may book in a chat to get to know you a bit more and walk you through the process.

Example if you recruit in cohorts or at specific times

We welcome new volunteers at certain times during the year.

We'll get in touch with you when we review the registration list.

We'll then discuss which roles are available and how you can support us.

Examples of checks for all types of opportunities

To become a volunteer, you will need to:

  • provide proof of identity and some utility bills
  • go through a standard or enhanced Disclosure and Barring Service (DBS) and occupational health checks
  • prove your right to work in the UK
  • provide details of your work history
  • give the details of two referees
