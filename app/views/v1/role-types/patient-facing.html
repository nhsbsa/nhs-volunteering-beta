Provide patient-facing companionship

You'll be supporting the medical and clinical staff in making patients' lives a little more comfortable.

Opportunities that might interest you

  • Patient companion or visitor
  • Mealtime support volunteer
  • Meet and greet volunteer
  • Refreshment trolley volunteer
  • Chaplaincy or pastoral support
  • Ward support volunteer

What your day could look like

  • Reading or chatting to patients
  • Engaging patients in social activities
  • Helping patients keep in contact with their families
  • Supporting patients at meal time
  • Welcoming patients and visitors and sign posting them to services

Consider these opportunities if you

  • Enjoy direct contact with people
  • Like meeting new people
  • Want to use your good communication and interpersonal skills
What you don't have to do

You may be in direct contact with patients but you won't engage in any clinical or therapeutic work.

You won't have to perform any tasks that require any medical expertise or training. Those tasks will remain under the responsibility of trained health and social care professionals.
