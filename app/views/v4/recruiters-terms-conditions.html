Use of NHS Volunteering

NHS Volunteering is a service centrally funded by NHS England. The following organisations based in England can use NHS Volunteering without charge:

  • NHS organisations, including NHS trusts, ambulance trusts, GP practices
  • Voluntary, community or social enterprise (VCSE) partner organisations

The NHS Volunteering team may consider other requests to use the NHS Volunteering service. They may grant access where appropriate.

When reviewing requests, the NHS Volunteering team will consider some of these points:

  • the impact to NHS health and social care services
  • the volunteering and recruiting organisation experience
  • the cost to the public
  • removing any potential for financial gain
  • the potential loss of data or reporting capability

This list is not exhaustive.

You agree to use NHS Volunteering to advertise volunteering opportunities that:

  • relate to live volunteering roles that support the provision of healthcare in England
  • are commissioned or funded by NHS England, Department of Health and Social Care, or any other body forming part of the NHS in England
  • have not been filled prior to advertisement

You agree not to use NHS Volunteering in any way that is unlawful, illegal or detrimental to the NHS Volunteering service, the organisations using NHS Volunteering, the volunteers, NHS Business Services Authority or NHS England.

And you agree not to:

  • use NHS Volunteering for commercial purposes without obtaining our prior written agreement
  • copy, reproduce, distribute, republish, download, display, post or transmit in any form or by any means any of the content on NHS Volunteering except as permitted above

You are responsible for the accuracy of any information you present on NHS Volunteering. You must not publish any material that:

  • is in violation of any law or regulation that is enforceable in the United Kingdom
  • is illegal, inappropriate or offensive - this includes material for internal use as well as for external publication
  • contains viruses or other malicious software or any tools designed to compromise the security of users, websites and/or systems

You must ensure that your use of the service promotes fair, open competition and equal opportunities for all volunteers.

You must not sell access to NHS Volunteers to any other organisation or user.

Management of user access

You must designate a minimum of one super user for your organisation and other super users to replace the main super user when unavailable.

The super users in your organisation are responsible for:

  • managing and maintaining user access within your organisation, including setting up user accounts and access rights
  • ensuring that approved users are employees of your organisation or of a shared service that manages volunteering recruitment on your behalf
  • ensuring that user accounts are set up using your organisation’s email domain address or the domain address of a shared service that manages recruitment on your behalf - for security reasons personal email addresses are not permitted
  • ensuring that the user accounts are reviewed and, where necessary, deleted on an annual basis
  • ensuring that users within your organisations are adequately trained
  • offering support to users within your organisation and escalate to the NHS Volunteering team when needed
  • organising and co-ordinating super user and housekeeping activities within your organisation
  • cascading any messages sent by the NHS Volunteering team

The NHS Volunteering team will support all users with queries.

You must ensure that users know how to contact your organisation’s super users for support.

Enforcement of the policy

Failure to adhere to this policy will be deemed a breach of service and may lead to your NHS Volunteering access being suspended or permanently withdrawn.

The NHS Volunteering team reserves the right to investigate any suspected violation of this policy. You are obliged to assist in any investigation undertaken.

The NHS Volunteering team may decide to take action, with or without notice if it finds, at its sole discretion, that you or any third-party organisation have failed to adhere to this policy. This includes, but is not restricted to:

  • contacting you to request compliance with this policy
  • removal of any material, including but not limited to open volunteer adverts
  • restriction or suspension of your access to parts of the service suspension or termination of your account

If you gain access to the service by providing false data during the sign-up process your account will immediately be suspended.

The NHS Volunteering team does not check or approve advertisements placed on the service but reserves the right to remove, without notice, any advertisement which breaches legislative requirements, generates complaints from volunteers, appears misleading or inappropriate or in breach of the terms of this policy.

How long we keep data

We will keep:

  • data on NHS Volunteering for 24 months from the date the applicant submits their application
  • an audit log for 24 months to allow NHS Volunteering processes to be independently checked

This will allow you to revisit volunteering opportunities and volunteer application data.

It is your responsibility to ensure that any data required after 24 months is held locally, off the service, and you make volunteers aware of your data retention policy in your privacy policy.

You must make volunteers aware that pseudonymised information associated with their application will be shared with NHSBSA for the purpose of volunteering insight and reporting which will be shared with relevant parties such as NHS England and the Department of Health and Social Care.

You must display your organisation's privacy notice on your volunteering adverts.
