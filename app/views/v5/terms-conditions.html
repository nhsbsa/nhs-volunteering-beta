Communication

You must provide a valid personal email address when you register with NHS Volunteering and for each registration you submit.

If you supply an incorrect email address you will not receive messages related to your account or your registration.

We do not guarantee reliability of email communications. You should contact the recruiting organisation if you have any concerns.

Passwords

You must not share any passwords to access NHS Volunteering with anyone.

If you think there has been a breach of security, report it via email to the NHS Volunteering team.

Registrations

You might need to complete a form to register for a volunteering opportunity through NHS Volunteering.

The information you provide in your registration form and any other material you enter into NHS Volunteering must be truthful, complete and accurate. It must not include any material that is false, misleading, defamatory, discriminatory, threatening, offensive, abusive, likely to cause someone anxiety or distress, encourages violence or racial or religious hatred, blasphemous, pornographic, in breach of confidence, in breach of privacy, unlawful, harassing, libelous, harmful, vulgar, obscene, or otherwise objectionable.

The declaration you agree to on submission of the form is valid and binding.

The recruiting organisation will assess registrations and any accompanying attachments. They will take all decisions relating to the opportunities and not us. If you have queries relating to an advert or your registration you should contact the recruiting organisation.

If you register through NHS Volunteering, the organisation which advertises the opportunity will have access to your information and any other supporting material. They will not have access to any data collected for monitoring purposes.
