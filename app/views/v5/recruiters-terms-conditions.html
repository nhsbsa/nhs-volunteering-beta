Terms and conditions for recruiters

This page and any pages it links to explains NHS Volunteering's terms of use.

You must agree to these to use NHS Volunteering.

Who we are

NHS Volunteering is run by NHS Business Services Authority on behalf of NHS England and will be referred to as ‘we’ from now on.

Definitions

Data controller - as defined in Data Protection Legislation and Article 26 of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

Data processer - as defined in Data Protection Legislation and the definitions of 'processed' and 'processing' shall apply accordingly

NHS Volunteering team - refers to the NHS Business Services Authority and NHS England, who manage and deliver NHS Volunteering

Offline - an activity that is completed outside of either the NHS Volunteering system, your own applicant tracking system or a third-party applicant tracking system i.e. applicant progress being tracked by a spreadsheet or another manual process

Online- an activity recorded in an online solution such as internal or third-party applicant tracking system

Organisation - an organised group of people with a particular purpose, such as a business or government department

Recruiting organisation - the organisation that is requiring a volunteering opportunity to be filled

Service - the NHS Volunteering recruitment platform and helpdesk services

Super user - a user with administration rights for the recruiting organisation and the responsibility for first line query resolution

Secondary user - TBC

Third party - a person, group, or suppliers involved in the process besides the two predominant parties

Volunteer - an individual searching and applying for a volunteering opportunity

You and your - refers to your organisation as a user of the NHS Volunteering service

Intellectual property and permitted use

NHS Business Services Authority is the owner or licensee of all intellectual property rights in NHS Volunteering and the material published on it. Those works are protected by copyright laws and treaties worldwide. All such rights are reserved.

NHS Volunteering is a is centrally funded service.

The following organisations based in England can use NHS Volunteering without charge:

  • NHS organisations, including NHS trusts, ambulance trusts, GP practices
  • Voluntary, community or social enterprise (VCSE) partner organisations

This list is not exhaustive.

The NHS Volunteering team may consider additional requests to use the NHS Volunteering service and grant access where appropriate.

When reviewing requests, the NHS Volunteering team will consider some of these points:

  • the impact to NHS health and social care services
  • the volunteering and recruiting organisation experience
  • the cost to the public
  • removing any potential for financial gain
  • the potential loss of data or reporting capability

This list is not exhaustive.

You agree to use NHS Volunteering to advertise volunteering opportunities that:

  • relate to live volunteering roles that support the provision of healthcare in England
  • are commissioned or funded by NHS England, Department of Health and Social Care, or any other body forming part of the NHS in England
  • have not been filled prior to advertisement

You agree not to use NHS Volunteering in any way that is unlawful, illegal or detrimental to the NHS Volunteering service, the organisations using NHS Volunteering, the volunteers, NHS Business Services Authority or NHS England.

And you agree not to:

  • use NHS Volunteering for commercial purposes without obtaining prior written policy from NHS Business Services Authority
  • copy, reproduce, distribute, republish, download, display, post or transmit in any form or by any means any of the content on NHS Volunteering except as permitted above

You may print and download extracts from NHS Volunteering for personal non-commercial use, as long as you don’t:

  • change any documents or related graphics in any way
  • separate any graphics from accompanying text
  • remove any copyright and trade mark notices

You are responsible for the accuracy of any information you present on NHS Volunteering. You must not publish any material that:

  • is in violation of any law or regulation that is enforceable in the United Kingdom
  • is illegal, inappropriate or offensive - this includes material for internal use as well as material intended for external publication
  • contains viruses or other malicious software or any tools designed to compromise the security of users, websites and/or systems

You are allowed to make paper applications available for volunteers that are unable to submit an online application.

You must ensure that your use of the service promotes fair, open competition and equal opportunities for all volunteers.

You must not sell access to NHS Volunteers to any other organisation or user.

Intellectual property and permitted use

You have the option to receive applications through an external link to:

  • your third party recruitment system
  • your organisation’s website
  • a digital form
  • a digital document

When using an external link, you are responsible for ensuring the accuracy of the link and that it is not detrimental to NHS Volunteering service.
