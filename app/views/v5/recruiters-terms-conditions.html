Terms and conditions for recruiters

This page and any pages it links to explains NHS Volunteering's terms of use.

You must agree to these to use NHS Volunteering.

Who we are

NHS Volunteering is run by NHS Business Services Authority on behalf of NHS England and will be referred to as ‘we’ from now on.

Definitions

Data controller - as defined in Data Protection Legislation and Article 26 of the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

Data processer - as defined in Data Protection Legislation and the definitions of 'processed' and 'processing' shall apply accordingly

NHS Volunteering team - refers to the NHS Business Services Authority and NHS England, who manage and deliver NHS Volunteering

Offline - an activity that is completed outside of either the NHS Volunteering system, your own applicant tracking system or a third-party applicant tracking system i.e. applicant progress being tracked by a spreadsheet or another manual process

Online- an activity recorded in an online solution such as internal or third-party applicant tracking system

Organisation - an organised group of people with a particular purpose, such as a business or government department

Recruiting organisation - the organisation that is requiring a volunteering opportunity to be filled

Service - the NHS Volunteering recruitment platform and helpdesk services

Super user - a user with administration rights for the recruiting organisation and the responsibility for first line query resolution

Secondary user - TBC

Third party - a person, group, or suppliers involved in the process besides the two predominant parties

Volunteer - an individual searching and applying for a volunteering opportunity

You and your - refers to your organisation as a user of the NHS Volunteering service

Intellectual property and permitted use

NHSBSA is the owner or licensee of all intellectual property rights in NHS Volunteering and the material published on it. Those works are protected by copyright laws and treaties worldwide. All such rights are reserved.

NHS Volunteering is a is centrally funded service.

The following organisations based in England can use NHS Volunteering without charge:

  • NHS organisations, including NHS trusts, ambulance trusts, GP practices
  • Voluntary, community or social enterprise (VCSE) partner organisations
